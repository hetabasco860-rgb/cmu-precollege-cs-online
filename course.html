Course Format

The program is delivered fully online across 20 structured sessions. Instruction emphasizes conceptual foundations, programming practice, and project-oriented skill development.

Students progress through guided instruction, applied exercises, and milestone check-ins designed to support consistent improvement over the duration of the program.

Learning Objectives

By the end of the program, students are expected to:

  • Demonstrate proficiency in structured programming and algorithmic thinking.
  • Apply core concepts from AP Computer Science A and AP Computer Science Principles.
  • Use computational models to analyze problems and justify design decisions.
  • Develop and present a complete final project with clear documentation.

Curriculum Outline

The curriculum spans the AP CS foundations and extends into advanced topics. Specific emphasis areas may include:

  • Programming fundamentals and program design
  • Data structures and algorithmic problem solving
  • Computing systems, data, networks, and societal impacts
  • Advanced topics beyond the AP curriculum (selected based on cohort readiness)

Certificate Requirements

Students who successfully complete the program and submit the Final Project will receive a Certificate of Completion.

The Final Project is evaluated from a single official submission. Expectations, sample project directions, and evaluation criteria are provided on the Projects page.
